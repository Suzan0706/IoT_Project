Open data governance & metadata standard

How ARU's IUC research community describes, shares and stewards IoT sensor datasets — aligned to Dublin Core and the FAIR data principles, extended for sensor-specific context.

1. Open data policy

All IoT sensor datasets generated under VLIR-UOS / IUC project funding at ARU are open by default. Researchers register datasets in the IoT system at the point of deployment; access controls and embargoes are exceptions, requested in writing and time-bound to a maximum of 24 months.

Datasets containing personal data, location data linked to vulnerable populations, or commercially sensitive observations are reviewed by the ICT & SSIC stewardship board before publication and may be released under restricted-access licenses.

2. Guiding principles

Findable

Every dataset has a persistent identifier (DOI on approval) and rich, indexed metadata.

Accessible

Metadata is always public; data files are retrievable via a stated protocol.

Interoperable

Dublin Core + IoT extension; SI units; ISO 8601 dates; WGS84 coordinates.

Reusable

Open licenses preferred (CC0, CC BY 4.0); provenance and quality are explicit.

3. Metadata schema

The ARU IoT metadata schema combines the 15 Dublin Core Metadata Element Set elements with an iot: and aru: extension namespace for sensor-specific context.

Field Dublin Core / extension Required Notes
title dc:title Required Concise, human-readable label.
description dc:description Required ≥ 50 words, plain-language abstract.
domain dc:subject Required Controlled list: Transport, Housing, Heritage, Energy, Water, Air Quality, Agriculture, Health.
sensorType iot:sensorType (ext.) Required Controlled list maintained by ICT & SSIC.
location dc:coverage Required Place name + lat/lon decimal degrees (WGS84).
startDate / endDate dc:date Required ISO 8601. Blank end = ongoing.
updateFrequency iot:samplingInterval (ext.) Required Human-readable (e.g. "1 min", "15 s").
units iot:units (ext.) Required SI preferred; mixed allowed if comma-separated.
qualityScore aru:qualityScore (ext.) Required 0.0–5.0; rubric defined below.
license dc:rights Required CC0, CC BY 4.0 preferred.
format dc:format Optional CSV, Parquet, JSON, NetCDF, …
doi dc:identifier Optional Reserved on approval.
researcher dc:creator Required Lead researcher; ORCID recommended.
department dc:contributor Required ARU department or partner institution.
iucProject aru:iucProject (ext.) Required VLIR-UOS / IUC project code.

4. Data quality rubric

The aru:qualityScore field uses a 0.0–5.0 scale, assigned by the lead researcher and verified at stewardship review.

5.0 Reference-grade: Independently calibrated, peer-reviewed, gap-free.
4.0 Production-grade: Calibrated, gap-checked, documented preprocessing.
3.0 Research-grade: Raw + basic QC; known outliers documented.
2.0 Pilot-grade: Raw data with acknowledged gaps; use with caution.
1.0 Experimental: Prototype deployment; not suitable for downstream conclusions.

5. Dataset lifecycle

01

Registration

Researcher submits metadata via the system registration form at the start of deployment.

02

Stewardship review

ICT & SSIC checks completeness, controlled-vocabulary use and license compatibility.

03

Publication

Dataset becomes discoverable; DOI is reserved; download link is published.

04

Maintenance

Researcher updates end date, record count and quality score as deployment evolves.

05

Retirement

After 7 years of inactivity, dataset is archived; metadata remains permanently citable.

6. Stewardship & contacts

Day-to-day stewardship of the IoT system is delegated to the ICT & SSIC office, with policy oversight from the postgraduate research committee. The VLIR-UOS reporting team draws on the analytics dashboard for annual reporting cycles.

Questions, policy exceptions and embargo requests: iot-atlas@aru.example
